Programs > FIBL

Digital training material on organic agriculture for farmers in East Africa

This pilot project aims at increasing access to knowledge on organic farming of farmers through the digitalization of content and testing of digital trainings.

The project makes use of already available training resources such as the African Organic Manual produced by FiBL & IFOAM and the Infonet-Biovision, which are tailored to small-scale African farmers. The provided information is transformed into text messages and a smartphone app, making the resources more accessible for farmers.

For the SMS training, FiBL partners with Arifu, who sends short messages about organic farming to thousands of farmers, Fesy Rwanda who translates the content of the message and ROAM who monitors and guide facilitate the implementation/learning with the use of messages.

The messages are divided into modules so that farmers learn in an easy and fun way. The learners follow an exemplary farmer, MUKANTWALI, throughout her process of becoming an organic farmer. Each module ends with a short test, and farmers are provided with links to resources to learn more.
