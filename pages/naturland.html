Programs > Naturland

Capacitating actors and increasing sustainability in the organic coffee value chain by supporting strategic development initiatives in Rwanda

As in many African countries, agriculture is the most important economic sector in Rwanda: it employs about 80% of the population and contributes 35% of the annual GDP. Since the early 1990s,organic agriculture (OA) has also developed in response to incentives and demand from the global organic market, led by the production of organic coffee and tea, However Organic agriculture has not yet been highly adopted in Rwanda.

Naturland, an international association for organic agriculture is currently active in various East African Community countries, with current and completed projects in Tanzania, Uganda, Burundi and Rwanda with an overall mission of strengthening smallholder production capacity, improving quality and productivity in line with better practice organic and agroforestry approaches, and contributing to the creation of fair and sustainable value chains that include significant environmental benefits.

The overall objective of the project in Rwanda is to promote skills development, organizational capacity building and adoption of best practices of Rwandan organic coffee farmers and strengthen their position in the organic value chain.

Target groups and beneficiaries

At farm level,the target groups are envisaged to be up to 1000 farmers in up to 4 groups and/or cooperatives. This work will be supported by up to 15 multipliers of skills and knowledge: the primary action will focus on these individuals that work with potential farmers and farmers’ groups/cooperatives. These multipliers could include advisers and extension workers, lead farmers, demonstration farmers, trade and processing cooperatives, etc

At national level,the target groups are envisaged to be up to 1000 farmers in up to 4 groups and/or cooperatives. This work will be supported by up to 15 multipliers of skills and knowledge: the primary action will focus on these individuals that work with potential farmers and farmers’ groups/cooperatives. These multipliers could include advisers and extension workers, lead farmers, demonstration farmers, trade and processing cooperatives, etc

At international level,the project will target both actors in the Rwandan value chain (cooperatives, processors, and traders) as well as international value chain actors interested in connecting with premium (Naturland-certified) organic coffee producers from Rwanda.
